Lives changed by Set & Thrive

These are real stories from real people who found the courage to set boundaries, walk away from toxic relationships, and build a life rooted in peace and self-worth.

Case study 01

"I didn't know I was allowed to leave."

Thandi's story — Toxic friendship

Toxic friendship Boundary workshops Cape Town
🧕
The situation

Thandi, 24, had been best friends with someone since high school. For years she gave everything to the friendship, showing up at 2am when things were hard, lending money she never got back, cancelling her own plans to be available. But when Thandi needed support after losing her job, her friend was nowhere to be found. Not a single call. Not one message.

When Thandi came to Set & Thrive she felt confused, guilty, and ashamed, as if ending the friendship would make her a bad person. She had never been taught that leaving a relationship that was hurting her was not betrayal. It was survival.

How Set & Thrive helped

Through our Boundary Workshops, Thandi learned to identify the signs of a one-sided friendship,the pattern of giving without receiving, the anxiety before seeing her friend, and the exhaustion afterwards. She learned that her discomfort was not weakness but wisdom.

We worked with Thandi on how to have an honest conversation, how to communicate what she needed, and how to gracefully distance herself when those needs were not met. She also joined one of our Time Circles where she connected with others in similar situations.

"Set & Thrive taught me that leaving a friendship that was draining me was not giving up on someone — it was choosing myself. For the first time I felt like I was allowed to do that."

— Thandi, 24, Cape Town
Case study 02

"He made me feel like I was too much — and never enough."

Naledi's story — Toxic relationship

Toxic relationship Boundary workshops Johannesburg
👩
The situation

Naledi, 27, spent three years in a relationship where her boundaries were constantly dismissed. Her partner would show up late with no apology, cancel plans without explanation, and make her feel guilty for having needs. Naledi convinced herself this was normal, that love meant tolerating everything.

She came to Set & Thrive after a close friend pointed out that she had stopped talking about anything except her relationship. She was exhausted, isolated, and had lost touch with who she was before she met him.

How Set & Thrive helped

Through our Boundary Workshops, Naledi learned to identify patterns of emotional dismissal, the minimising of her feelings, the lack of accountability, and the way her needs were consistently treated as inconveniences. She realised she had never been taught what healthy love actually looks like.

We worked with Naledi on rebuilding her sense of self outside of the relationship. She joined our Relationship Audit program and for the first time saw clearly what she had been accepting. She made the decision to leave and has not looked back since.

"I didn't even know I had the right to have boundaries. I thought love meant tolerating everything. Set & Thrive changed everything I believed about what I deserved."

— Naledi, 27, Johannesburg
Case study 03

"I was always the one reaching out. Always."

Sipho's story — One-sided friendship

One-sided friendship Time circles Cape Town
🧑
The situation

Sipho, 22, noticed that in most of his friendships he was always the one initiating. Always the one checking in. Always the one making plans. Always the one showing up. When he stopped reaching out as a test, most friendships went completely silent.

He came to Set & Thrive's Time Circle feeling invisible and deeply lonely — despite having many people he called friends. He questioned whether something was wrong with him, or whether he was simply too much effort for people to bother with.

How Set & Thrive helped

Through our Time Circles, Sipho connected with others who had experienced the same silent disappearing act. He learned that over-functioning in friendships was a pattern rooted in fear of abandonment — and that stepping back was not rejection but a healthy act of self-respect.

We helped Sipho set a personal standard — to match energy rather than exceed it. He invested his time in two deep friendships instead of many shallow ones and reported feeling less drained and more genuinely connected than ever before.

"Set & Thrive helped me see that stepping back was not giving up — it was finally giving people the chance to show me who they really were. Some showed up. Most didn't. Both answers were valuable."

— Sipho, 22, Delft Cape Town
